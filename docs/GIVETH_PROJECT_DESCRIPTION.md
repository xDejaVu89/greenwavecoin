## The Problem No One Talks About

Right now, 1.8 billion personal computers around the world are sitting idle. Fans spinning slowly. Processors doing nothing. Meanwhile, the biggest AI labs in the world are spending $50 million to $500 million on a single training run — renting compute from Amazon, Google, and Microsoft, who capture every dollar of that value.

Independent researchers, universities in developing countries, and open-source AI projects are simply priced out. They cannot afford to participate in cutting-edge AI research. The result? The people who will shape the future of AI are increasingly a small, well-funded club.

GreenWaveCoin exists to change that.

---

## What We Built

GreenWaveCoin (GWC) is a live, open-source protocol on Polygon Mainnet that turns idle consumer computers into a distributed AI research network. Here's how it works:

1. You download the worker (11MB, Windows — macOS and Linux coming soon)
2. Your computer receives micro-tasks from our open-source coordinator — small Neural Architecture Search jobs that test candidate AI model designs
3. Your CPU does the work while you're not using your computer
4. You earn GWC tokens, verified and paid out on-chain through a Merkle proof system — no middleman, no trust required

This is not a whitepaper. This is not a roadmap. This is running right now on Polygon Mainnet.

## Why You Should Care

If you believe AI should be built in public, by everyone — not just by the labs that can afford $500M training runs — then GreenWaveCoin is infrastructure for that future.

Every task completed by the network is a small piece of Neural Architecture Search: the process of discovering better, more efficient AI model designs. The results are not proprietary. They belong to the research commons. We are building a public-good supercomputer, one idle CPU at a time.

Here's how we compare to what already exists:

- BOINC and Folding@home ask workers to donate compute for free. GWC pays workers in tokens.
- Render, Akash, and io.net are GPU-only networks. GWC runs on any consumer CPU, expanding the addressable worker base by 10 to 100 times.
- Bittensor and Gensyn have complex validator systems with high hardware requirements. GWC works on a $300 laptop.

We are the lowest-barrier, highest-accessibility distributed compute network in Web3.
