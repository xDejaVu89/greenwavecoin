## 3. Project Description (Long)

### What is GreenWaveCoin?

GreenWaveCoin (GWC) is an open-source, permissionless DePIN (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Network) that coordinates idle consumer CPUs to perform Neural Architecture Search (NAS) — a computationally intensive AI research task that involves discovering optimal neural network designs by training and evaluating thousands of candidate architectures.

The network operates on Polygon Mainnet. Workers download a lightweight client (currently Windows, 11MB), connect to the open-source coordinator at api.greenwavecoin.com, receive micro-tasks, train small neural architectures locally, and submit results. Rewards are distributed via a Merkle proof claim system through the RewardEscrowV2 smart contract, with 4,985,000 GWC tokens (49.85% of total supply) pre-funded and locked in escrow.

### Why This Is a Public Good

**AI research is becoming increasingly centralized.** The cost of training frontier models has risen to $50M–$500M per run, effectively excluding independent researchers, universities in developing nations, and open-source AI projects from participating in cutting-edge neural architecture research. This concentration of compute creates a structural advantage for a small number of well-funded corporations, with downstream effects on who shapes AI development.

**GreenWaveCoin directly addresses this by:**

1. **Democratizing access to distributed compute** — Any person with a consumer PC can contribute CPU cycles and earn GWC tokens. No GPU required. No minimum hardware threshold. This is the lowest barrier to entry of any DePIN compute network.

2. **Advancing open AI research** — NAS results generated by the network are used to discover more efficient neural architectures. The research outputs are not proprietary; they contribute to the broader understanding of efficient AI design.

3. **Redistributing value to contributors** — Rather than cloud providers capturing 100% of the economic value of compute, GWC redistributes it to the individuals whose hardware actually performs the work.

4. **Building on public infrastructure** — The network runs on Polygon, a public blockchain with low fees and high throughput, ensuring that reward claims are accessible to workers globally without prohibitive transaction costs.

### Technical Architecture

The GreenWaveCoin network consists of three open-source components:

| Component | Description | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **GWC ERC-20 Token** | Fixed-supply token on Polygon Mainnet (10M total) | Live |
| **RewardEscrowV2** | Smart contract holding 4.985M GWC for worker rewards | Live, funded |
| **Coordinator Server** | Node.js API at api.greenwavecoin.com — dispatches NAS tasks, scores results | Live |
| **Worker Client** | Windows .exe (v1.0.4) — receives tasks, trains micro-architectures, submits results | Live, v1.0.4 |
| **Landing Site** | greenwavecoin.com — waitlist, leaderboard, worker download | Live on Vercel |

All components are open-source and available at https://github.com/xDejaVu89/greenwavecoin.

### How Rewards Work

Workers earn GWC proportional to the number of tasks completed and their accuracy score. At the end of each epoch, the coordinator generates a Merkle tree of all worker allocations. Workers claim their rewards on-chain by submitting a Merkle proof to RewardEscrowV2 — no intermediary, no custodial risk, fully verifiable.

## 5. What Are You Building? What Problem Does It Solve?

**The Problem:** AI training costs are doubling every 9 months. A single frontier model run costs $50M–$500M in centralized cloud compute. Neural Architecture Search — the process of discovering optimal AI model designs — is embarrassingly parallelizable across thousands of nodes, but only accessible to well-funded labs. Meanwhile, 1.8 billion consumer PCs worldwide sit idle for 80–90% of their uptime.

**The Solution:** GreenWaveCoin coordinates those idle CPUs into a distributed NAS supercomputer. Workers contribute compute, earn GWC tokens, and the network collectively advances AI architecture research that would otherwise require millions of dollars in cloud spend.

**Why Now:** DePIN is the fastest-growing sector in Web3. Comparable projects (Bittensor $4B, Render $3B) have demonstrated that decentralized compute networks can achieve significant scale. GWC is the first project to focus specifically on CPU-based NAS — a vertical with 1.8 billion potential workers and no incumbent.

## 8. Who Is Your Target Audience?

GreenWaveCoin serves two groups:

**Workers (Supply Side):** Anyone with a consumer PC who wants to earn cryptocurrency passively. No technical knowledge required. No GPU needed. The target demographic is the same as BOINC/Folding@home — people who want to contribute their idle compute to a meaningful cause and be compensated for it.

**AI Researchers (Demand Side):** Independent researchers, universities, and open-source AI projects that need NAS compute but cannot afford cloud pricing. The network provides a cost-effective alternative to AWS/GCP for architecture search workloads.

---

## 9. What Makes Your Project Unique?

Three differentiators set GreenWaveCoin apart from existing DePIN compute networks:

**CPU-only, no GPU required.** Render, Akash, and io.net all target GPU compute. GWC is the only major DePIN network that runs entirely on consumer CPUs. This expands the addressable worker base by 10–100× compared to GPU-only networks.

**Specific AI research task (NAS).** Rather than being a general-purpose compute marketplace, GWC focuses on Neural Architecture Search — a specific, high-value AI research task that is well-suited to distributed CPU computation. This focus enables better task optimization and more meaningful research outputs.

**Fully on-chain reward verification.** Workers can independently verify their reward allocation using the Merkle proof system before claiming. There is no trusted intermediary in the reward path — the smart contract enforces all payouts.
